Studying the Bible: Colossians 2

Feb 25 2015 at 18:30 UTC

Let's study Colossians 2.

Observations

First, make some observations. What does the passage say? Possibly things that are emphasized or repeated. (Quick short statements.)

  • Paul talked about Christ, again.
  • Paul mentions having a new life in Christ and not focusing on the things of this world that pass away.
  • He speaks of us not being under the law.
  • Don't be deceived.
  • Reminds of how we were saved, through contrast with the society of the world vs the society of the Church in Christ.
  • He mentions legalism.

Theme

Now we need to interpret what we've read. What does the passage mean? Look at cross references. Study the culture. Draw conclusions as to the meaning of the passage. Use the observations if they are helpful, but it is not required. What are one to three main (theme) ideas from this passage?

Working toward the theme...

  1. How to live the Christian life?
  2. Living the Christian life without the Law.
  3. Focusing on Christ and not other restrictions.
  4. What about the first part? It's backdrop for the chapter.
  5. Newness of life.
  6. Going from a legalistic life to one of Grace with Christ.
  7. How to move to a life of Grace through Jesus from a legalistic life.

When these thoughts combined, we came up with:

In Christ we can move from a legalistic life to one of Grace.

Four things you can do with truth are explain, prove, restate, and apply it.

Explain the Truth

  • What does it mean to live a legalistic life?
    • I'll get those later. I'll throw some guesses down.
    • Focusing one what we should do.
    • Working very hard, but failing.
  • What does it mean to be in Christ?
    • To have a heart that seeks Him.
    • To have faith in Christ.
    • To walk in Him.
    • Being abundant in thanksgiving.
    • Buried with Him in baptism and raised with Him in faith.
    • Being renewed by Christ.
    • We have died to the World with Christ.
    • Allowing Christ to be the head.
    • Putting your hope in Him.
  • What does it mean to live a life of Grace?
    • Dependence on Jesus.
    • United in love?
    • To live a life of freedom! (See Galatians 5:1)
    • Living with the awareness of your salvation and showing that freedom to others.
    • Allowing Jesus to take our burden.
    • Live in the free-life that Jesus made possible.

Prove the Truth

  • How can you prove that in Christ can set us free?
    • Changed lives of believers.
    • Thankfulness of believers.
    • The powerful resurrection of Jesus Christ from the grave.

Restate the Truth

  • How can you restate the truth?
    • Jesus sets us free.
    • The law is but a shadow of Jesus in its ability to save us.

Apply the Truth

  • See the last step for how to apply the truth.

Tone

How does the passage make me feel? Look for the emotion of the passage.

  • FREE!
  • Overwhelmed with the truth.
  • Inspired to share.
  • Encouraged!
  • Awakened to the truth.

Application

This is the goal of all the studying we've done up to this point. What do we do?

  1. How does the truth revealed here affect my relationship with God?
  2. How does the truth revealed here affect my relationship with others?
  3. How does the truth revealed here affect me?
  4. How does the truth revealed here affect my response to Satan?

Our thoughts:

  • Colossians 2:23 - Don't rely on legalistic rules because they are of no value in stopping the desires of the flesh. But instead, rely on Jesus Christ.
  • Live a life of thankfulness toward God in response to the grace He has shown us through Jesus Christ.
  • When Satan tempts us, using our freedom to choose to ignore him and follow our Lord, Jesus Christ.
